  • Design Process

    I've always been inspired by Maya Lin's design for the Vietnam Memorial. It is powerful in many ways, I appreciate the design most for it's simplicity and impressiveness in the sheer number of names. The memorial forces the viewer to consider each one of the names writted on the wall and attach a life to it.

    The number of drafts and ideas on how to visualize the theme of the future were numerous. The word future means something entirely different to everyone. The predicment was how to show the future, such a broad idea in a way the audience of tempo could relate to.

  • Design Process

    I decided to ask hundreds of students to complete a simple statement the future is... The variety of interpretations on how to answer the question were amazing. They were sincere, optimistic, funny and clever.

  • Design Process

    After a great amount of typing the future is...into the computer and a great amount of copy editing, the text was ready to be incorporated into the cover. Text on the page alone didn't seem to cut it though, it was missing something to draw the viewer into the page. The Vietnam Memorial adds intrigue based on the placement of granite jutting out of the earth and the reflective nature of the material. I added an anonymous face to lure the reader in and break up the monotony of lines the text created. The tempo staff and I received such a great number of responses to our question that the cover imagery was extended into the contents page.
